# Commonly asked questions following Exam Board results

## **How do I find out about my resit?**

If you are required to resit an assessment, information will be on your Blackboard. If you are unsure, please raise an enquiry on the Student Portal. If you are required to resit an exam, there should be a link on your exam board letter to the timetable. It may take a couple of weeks before the timetables are released, so please regularly check for updates.

## **I have 3 options on my examination board letter, what do they mean?**

We usually see students offered 3 options of resitting failed modules only, repeat whole level/stage or exit the programme with a lower award if eligible.

There will be a deadline to confirm which option you are selecting. These can be either 5 or 10 working days, so it it important to check. It is important to do this, as usually the university will allocate the lower award if they do not hear from you.

What do the options mean?

**Resitting failed modules only:** This is where you only resit the modules that you have not passed. You will need to check if this requires teaching. You can do this by speaking to your programme team. If you are an international student you would need to speak to the university about any impacts this may have on your visa.

**Repeating whole stage:**This is where you repeat all the modules including ones which you have already passed. You would need to confirm the fees associated for this, but this usually entails a full year of tuition fees.

**Exit programme with a lower award:** This is where you would leave the university with the lower award if you were eligible with the credits you have already obtained

## **My exam board results have been deferred due to an ongoing Academic Misconduct investigation, why?**

This indicates that an academic misconduct investigation is currently in progress. If you have recently attended a Stage 1 meeting and all concerns were resolved, please contact the university to inquire about when you can expect to receive updated information. If you are awaiting a Stage 1 or Stage 2 meeting, please be advised that you will need to wait until the process is concluded. Should you have any concerns regarding this matter, please reach out to your programme team.

## **Some of my marks are missing or incorrect, what can I do?**

If you believe that a mark is missing or incorrect from your examination board letter, you may wish to contact Ask4Help and provide a screenshot of the original mark displayed on Blackboard. Please also include the module code and name. This university [article](https://myportal.northumbria.ac.uk/Help-and-support/My-Programme/Results-and-Awards/Querying-your-academic-results) sets out steps for students to take for incorrect and/or missing marks.

## **I am so close to the next boundary, what can I do?**

We can provide some general guidance on this and if you’d like more specific advice to your case, I’d suggest that you query your results with the Ask4Help team via ‘Raising an Enquiry’ on your Student Portal or visiting in person at Student Central, Coach Lane library or at the Ask4help desk on the ground floor at London campus

Which marks are considered borderline? 

- 48 and 49
- 58 and 59
- 68 and 69

The award of a higher-class degree depends on how many of your 120 credits is to be applied at level 6 are marked at the higher level. So, if you’re in this borderline range and the half your credits (rules apply for marks ending in 8 or 9) are at the higher level, your award should be at that higher level. Further details can be found in [ARTA addendum May 2025](https://northumbria-cdn.azureedge.net/-/media/university-policies/policy-master-versions/arta-addendum-may-2025.pdf?modified=20250428114620)

You can find details of your marks and the number of credits for each module on your examination board letter. Have a look at your results and see if you can work out how many credits were achieved at the higher level. If you are unsure if you would like to send us your examination board letter and we can look for you. You can email the Advice Team [su.advice@northumbria.ac.uk](mailto:su.advice@northumbria.ac.uk)

## **I want a re-mark. How do I get this?**

Unfortunately, students are unable to request a re-mark. This is mentioned in the [Handbook of Student Regulations](https://www.northumbria.ac.uk/about-us/leadership-governance/vice-chancellors-office/legal-services-team/handbook-of-student-regulations/) and in their [Student Guidance](https://northumbria-cdn.azureedge.net/-/media/handbook-of-student-regulations/forms-and-guidance/taught-forms-and-guidance-2025-26/student-guide-to-submitting-an-academic-appeal-taught.docx?modified=20250725134527) (This link will download a word document)

The university detail that *“The appeals procedure is not a means of requesting a re-mark. Students must demonstrate they have grounds for an appeal.”*

Common outcome from successful appeals is granting students a resit when they have not already been granted this opportunity.

## **I think my marks don't reflect the work I put in.**

We are sorry to hear that you are unhappy with your marks, please see our question above.

The University operates a robust marking and moderation process where both internal and external examiners have been involved in checking marks to ensure consistency and fair marking.

Students can’t challenge a mark because they feel it is low or unfair or because it is their own judgement that their work is worth a higher mark.  This is called a question of academic judgment and staff marking work are qualified and have the expertise to mark students’ work.   This also means that students can’t challenge a mark through the Academic Appeal Procedure on the basis that they feel that their work is of higher quality.

## **I am unhappy with my marks/feedback, what can I do?**

If you are unhappy with your marks or feedback, it may be helpful for you to get in touch with the marker of your work to see if they can explain their view to you. If they identify an error or issue, this could then be corrected, or it may be that they could add context to something that isn’t obvious in their comments.

I would advise you contact your programme team and if you are unable to speak to them in person, you could raise an enquiry on your Student Portal via the blue Ask4help button and ask someone to contact you.

This is not a basis of appeal.

## **I have an accepted PEC, why do I have a resit?**

A common outcome of an accepted PEC is granting students a first sit (uncapped) resit. An accepted PEC does not change your mark or disregard the universities’ requirement to pass the module. Students are required to complete and pass all modules in the academic year to be able to progress and/or receive your final award.
